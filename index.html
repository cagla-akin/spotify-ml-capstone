Exploratory Data Analysis

Key Finding: Audio Features Alone Don't Explain Popularity

Audio features like danceability, energy, and tempo have weak individual correlations with popularity (max ~0.30). The real driver is something else entirely.

Correlation: Artist Avg Popularity ↔ Track Popularity

0.92

A track's popularity is almost entirely predicted by how popular the artist already is — not how the song sounds.

By engineering an artist average popularity feature (mean of each artist's top tracks), the model's R² jumped from 0.47 → 0.86 — a near doubling of explanatory power.

Model Results

Four Models, One Clear Winner

All models were trained on an 80/20 train-test split and evaluated using R² and RMSE. None showed significant overfitting (train/test gap < 0.03).

Feature Importance (Random Forest)

What Actually Drives Popularity?

97% of the model's predictive power comes from just two features. All 13 audio features combined account for only ~3%.

97%

of predictive power from year of release + artist popularity alone.

~3%

from all 13 audio features combined — danceability, energy, tempo, valence, and more.

Sound matters less than brand. A great-sounding song from an unknown artist will almost always underperform a mediocre one from a superstar.
